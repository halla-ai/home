Cheju Halla University met with Joshua Bae, Vice President of Saltlux America, in Silicon Valley to discuss AI technology integration for the RISE project. The meeting served as an opportunity to further strengthen the partnership recently established with Saltlux Korea and to expand the global AI education and research network.

![Visit to Saltlux America](/images/posts/20250110/goover-2.jpeg)

Vice President Joshua Bae offered insightful advice on how to transform complex data into actionable knowledge. In-depth discussions were held on the latest AI technology trends and educational methodologies from Silicon Valley, further reinforcing existing partnerships including those with Saltlux and Ploonet.

Cheju Halla University stated, "Through this connection with Silicon Valley, we have taken another step forward in developing Jeju as a hub for AI education and innovation. We will leverage these global partnerships to provide our students with better educational opportunities and contribute to building an AI innovation ecosystem in the Jeju region."

![Tour of Saltlux research facilities](/images/posts/20250110/goover-4.jpeg)

Saltlux America expressed, "We are pleased to be able to share the latest AI technology and expertise from Silicon Valley through our collaboration with Cheju Halla University," and hoped to "continue developing innovative AI education programs through ongoing cooperation."

![Discussions on cooperation plans](/images/posts/20250110/goover-5.jpeg)

Building on the outcomes of this Silicon Valley visit, Cheju Halla University plans to further develop its global-standard AI education and research programs.
