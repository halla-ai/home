Cheju Halla University is moving forward with cooperation with Pulse9 to strengthen AI-based immersive content education and research. This agreement is aimed at promoting mutual development and contributing to educational and cultural innovation in the Jeju region, and is expected to create new value for the local community by combining the resources and expertise of both institutions.

The key contents of the cooperation are as follows:

1. **Joint research and development to generate outcomes for the RISE (Regional Innovation System & Education) project**
2. **Development and operation of interactive avatar and docent systems**
3. **Production and operation of EDM/K-pop fusion performance content**
4. **Development and operation of locally specialized content**

This cooperation is part of an effort to strengthen the university's educational programs and provide innovative cultural experiences to the local community. Cheju Halla University President Kim Sunghoon noted, "We expect the university's educational environment to be significantly enhanced through this cooperation." Pulse9 CEO Park Jieun also emphasized the importance of the cooperation, calling it "an important opportunity to contribute to regional innovation and development."

This partnership is expected to play an important role in cultivating local talent and building an AI-based innovation ecosystem in Jeju. Additional news on upcoming joint projects and initiatives will be shared as they develop.
