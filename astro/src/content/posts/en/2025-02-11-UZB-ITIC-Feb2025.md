A delegation from Cheju Halla University (led by Professor Young Joon Lee) visited the Tashkent IT Investment Center (ITIC) to discuss AI education innovation and technology startup support measures. The meeting covered a variety of cooperation plans to strengthen the AI education and startup ecosystem.

## Key Discussion Points

1. **AI Education Program Development**: Development of AI prompt engineering and practical application courses
2. **Startup Innovation Support**: Building a student entrepreneurship support system in IT, AI, blockchain, and fintech
3. **Technology Exchange**: Establishing a framework for sharing technology and knowledge between Korea and Uzbekistan
4. **Joint Research Projects**: Advancing research and cooperation projects on emerging technologies

At this signing ceremony, both institutions reaffirmed their commitment to cooperating for AI technology innovation and support for next-generation technology startups. In particular, they agreed to collaborate closely with a focus on comprehensive support for student entrepreneurship and the establishment of specialized AI education programs.

The meeting concluded with an exchange of commemorative gifts, with both sides pledging to continue cooperation in contributing to an ecosystem that fosters AI innovation and entrepreneurship in Tashkent. This cooperation will serve as an important catalyst for further strengthening technology collaboration between Korea and Uzbekistan.
