The 'IJCAI 2024 Sustainable Transition with Artificial Intelligence (STAI) Workshop,' hosted by the Jeju Regional Innovation Platform Intelligent Services Business Division and co-organized by Cheju Halla University and KAIST, concluded successfully. This workshop was a meaningful gathering where researchers, practitioners, and experts from around the world came together to discuss the future of sustainable solutions through AI.

Of particular note was the poster research presented by students Yang Pilseong and Lee Kyubum under the guidance of Professor Young Joon Lee of our department. The study, titled "Enhancing Promotional Review Detection in Naver Restaurant Reviews: Integrating Large Language Models with Location-Based Data in Jeju," attracted the attention of participants as a real-world AI application case reflecting the unique characteristics of the Jeju region. This was a good example demonstrating that our department's research focuses on developing practical AI solutions tailored to regional characteristics.

Main Contents:

1. Research Presentation by the Department of AI at Cheju Halla University

   - Presenters: Students Yang Pilseong, Lee Kyubum (Advisor: Professor Young Joon Lee)
   - Topic: "Enhancing Promotional Review Detection in Naver Restaurant Reviews: Integrating Large Language Models with Location-Based Data in Jeju"

2. Keynote Speeches

   - Professor Markus Leippold: "The Role of Artificial Intelligence in Shaping Sustainable Finance"
   - Dr. Peter J. Morgan: "Fintech, Financial Inclusion, and Financial Literacy in Asia"

3. Panel Discussion

   - Topic: "Advancing AI Technology for Sustainable Development"
   - Moderator: Chairman Kim Sanghyup (Presidential Committee on Carbon Neutrality)

4. Best Paper Awards
   - Best Paper Award: Girmaw Abebe Tadesse
     Paper: "Decade-long Environmental Change Analysis in Namibia Using Deep Learning and Aerial Photography"
   - Best Student Paper Award: Han Sangyeon
     Paper: "Planetary Boundaries Meet Future Engineers: An AI-based Exploration"

This workshop covered a wide range of topics including sustainable finance, environmental monitoring, ethical AI deployment, and SME capacity building, demonstrating the multifaceted nature of AI and sustainability. In particular, the research presentations by our department's students served as a concrete example of how AI technology can contribute to local communities.

The Department of Artificial Intelligence at Cheju Halla University came away from this event with the conviction that AI will play an important role in creating a more sustainable and equitable world. Our university and department will continue to strive for sustainable innovation through AI, and will further dedicate ourselves to practical AI research and education that considers regional characteristics.

Finally, we extend our gratitude to everyone who worked hard to make this workshop a success. In particular, we express deep appreciation to Professor Young Joon Lee and students Yang Pilseong and Lee Kyubum for their excellent research achievements, and to the Jeju Regional Innovation Platform and the IJCAI-24 Organizing Committee for their support.

The Department of Artificial Intelligence at Cheju Halla University will continue its efforts for the advancement of AI technology and a sustainable future. We welcome the continued interest and support of all.
