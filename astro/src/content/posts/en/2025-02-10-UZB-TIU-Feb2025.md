A delegation from Cheju Halla University (led by Professor Young Joon Lee) visited Tashkent International University (TIU) in Uzbekistan to discuss collaboration for establishing an AI Education Center. The meeting covered in-depth discussions on key matters including curriculum design, technology infrastructure, and operational framework.

## Key Discussion Points

### 1. Student Selection and Support

- Select 20 motivated students with an IT background
- Target students aiming for employment upon graduation
- Establish support measures for improving English proficiency

### 2. Curriculum

- 16-week program structure
- Integration of Microsoft Azure and AWS certification courses
- Balanced delivery of theory and practical training

### 3. Technology Infrastructure

- State-of-the-art computer lab with advanced graphics capabilities
- Implementation of a comprehensive Learning Management System (LMS)
- Utilization of the latest AI tools including MS Copilot

### 4. Operational Framework

- Evening class schedule to maximize accessibility
- Systematic course progression and learning roadmap
- Clear pathways for student development and certification

Through this meeting, concrete implementation plans and timelines were confirmed so that the AI Education Center can become not merely a learning space, but an innovative platform that will lead Uzbekistan's future AI education.

Professor Young Joon Lee, head of the Cheju Halla University delegation, stated, "We will develop a program that leads AI education in Uzbekistan in collaboration with Tashkent International University," expressing hope that "this cooperation will become an important milestone contributing to technological advancement in Central Asia."

Building on these discussions, the AI Education Center is now preparing to welcome its first cohort of students ahead of its full launch. Continued efforts will be made through collaboration with TIU to cultivate regional AI talent and build a global education hub.
